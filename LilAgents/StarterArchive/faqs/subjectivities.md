## What Are Subjectivities?

In Excess & Surplus (E&S) lines insurance, **subjectivities** are conditions set by the insurer that must be satisfied before coverage can be bound. They verify that the applicant meets certain standards and has adequate risk management protocols in place.

Subjectivities typically require documentation such as:
- Financial records
- Operational procedures and protocols
- Certifications and licenses
- Service area details
- Claims history
- Signed applications and supplemental forms

## How to Complete Subjectivities in Pathpoint

### Step 1: Obtain Required Documentation
Gather all materials requested by the underwriter. These will be specified in the subjectivities checklist within the platform.

### Step 2: Ensure Proper Authorization
All forms must be **signed and dated by both you (the agent) and your insured**. Missing or incorrect signatures are the most common reason for rejection.

### Step 3: Submit Supplementals
Upload completed forms through the Pathpoint platform in the appropriate step of the bind workflow.

### Step 4: Monitor for Requests
Watch your inbox for emails with the subject line **ACTION NEEDED** if additional information is required.

### Step 5: Resubmit if Rejected
If a subjectivity is rejected by underwriting:
1. Review the rejection reason (highlighted in red in the portal)
2. Correct the issue (commonly a missing signature or wrong document)
3. Verify that you have uploaded the corrected form
4. Click **Resubmit** to send back to underwriting

## E-Sign vs. Manual Sign

**E-Sign (recommended):** The insured receives an email with a link to review and digitally sign all subjectivity documents. This is the fastest path to binding.

**Manual Sign:** Download the subjectivities packet, collect physical signatures from the insured, then upload the completed packet to the portal.

## Underwriter Review Process

After receiving your subjectivities, underwriting will:
1. Evaluate whether the submission meets binding criteria
2. Approve and issue the policy, or
3. Request revisions (ACTION NEEDED email), or
4. Decline coverage

Underwriters respond within **four business hours** of receiving a complete subjectivities submission.

## Common Reasons for Rejection
- Missing agent or insured signature
- Forms not dated
- Missing state-required digital verification stamps
- Incorrect or incomplete information on forms
- Wrong subjectivity packet uploaded (especially for Mix & Match quotes, which require two packets)
