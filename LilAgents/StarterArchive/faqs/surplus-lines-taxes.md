## Pathpoint Files Surplus Lines Taxes For You

**Pathpoint handles all surplus lines tax filings on your behalf.** You do not need to manage this separately or worry about the administrative burden of state surplus lines tax compliance.

After a policy is bound, Pathpoint Underwriting:
- Calculates the applicable surplus lines taxes for the state(s) involved
- Files all required forms and affidavits with the appropriate state regulators
- Maintains account compliance throughout the policy term

This is included as part of Pathpoint's standard service — there is no additional fee for tax filing.

## What Are Surplus Lines Taxes?

E&S (Excess & Surplus lines) insurance is placed with non-admitted carriers, which means state insurance departments require surplus lines brokers to file taxes and fees on each policy. These vary by state and typically range from 2%–6% of the premium. Common components include:

- **Surplus lines tax** — the primary state tax on non-admitted premium
- **Stamping fee** — charged by some state surplus lines offices (e.g., SLSOT in Texas, SLIP in Florida)
- **Service fee** — varies by state

These taxes and fees are included in the total premium presented in your Pathpoint quotes.
